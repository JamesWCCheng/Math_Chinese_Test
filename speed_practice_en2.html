🚀 SAT Math: Speed & Rate — Set 3

Word Problems & Multi-Step Rate Calculations

Rate = Distance ÷ Time
1Multiple Choice
Sarah runs a 10K race. She runs the first 6 km at 12 km/h and the last 4 km at 8 km/h. What is her average speed for the entire race?
Answer: (B) 10.0 km/h
Time: 6/12 + 4/8 = 0.5 + 0.5 = 1 hour. Avg = 10/1 = 10 km/h.
2Multiple Choice
A drone flies from A to B at 30 mph with a headwind of 10 mph, and returns with the same wind as a tailwind. If the distance is 60 miles, what is the total round-trip time?
Answer: (C) 4.5 hours
Going (headwind): 60/(30−10) = 60/20 = 3 hrs.
Return (tailwind): 60/(30+10) = 60/40 = 1.5 hrs. Total = 4.5 hours.
3Multiple Choice
Machine A produces 120 widgets per hour. Machine B produces 80 widgets per hour. How many minutes will it take both machines working together to produce 500 widgets?
Answer: (C) 150 minutes
Together: 200 widgets/hr. Time = 500/200 = 2.5 hrs = 150 minutes.
4Multiple Choice
A cyclist travels 12 miles at a certain speed. If she had traveled 2 mph faster, the trip would have taken 30 minutes less. What was her original speed?
Answer: (B) 6 mph
12/v − 12/(v+2) = 0.5. Multiply: 12(v+2)−12v = 0.5v(v+2). 24 = 0.5v²+v → v²+2v−48 = 0 → (v+8)(v−6) = 0 → v = 6 mph.
5Multiple Choice
Water drains from a full 600-gallon tank at 15 gallons per minute. At the same time, water flows in at 5 gallons per minute. How many minutes until the tank is empty?
Answer: (C) 60 minutes
Net drain rate = 15 − 5 = 10 gal/min. Time = 600/10 = 60 minutes.
6Multiple Choice
A commuter drives to work at 30 mph and returns the same route at 50 mph. If the total round-trip time is 1 hour 20 minutes, how far is it from home to work?
Answer: (B) 25 miles
d/30 + d/50 = 80/60 = 4/3 hrs. (5d+3d)/150 = 4/3. 8d = 200. d = 25 miles.
7Multiple Choice
A snail crawls at 0.03 mph. How many feet does the snail travel in 10 minutes? (1 mile = 5,280 feet)
Answer: (C) 26.4 ft
0.03 mph × (10/60) hr = 0.005 miles. 0.005 × 5280 = 26.4 feet.
8Multiple Choice
A car traveling at a constant speed passes mile marker 40 at 2:00 PM and mile marker 130 at 3:30 PM. At what speed is the car traveling?
Answer: (B) 60 mph
Distance = 130−40 = 90 miles. Time = 1.5 hours. Speed = 90/1.5 = 60 mph.
9Free ResponseSAT Grid-In
Pipe A fills a pool at 3 gallons/min. Pipe B fills at 5 gallons/min. The pool holds 960 gallons. If Pipe A runs alone for 40 minutes before Pipe B joins, how many total minutes from the start until the pool is full?
Answer: 145 minutes
After 40 min with A: 3×40 = 120 gal. Remaining: 840 gal. Both: 8 gal/min → 840/8 = 105 min. Total = 40+105 = 145.
10Multiple Choice
A package is shipped 420 miles. The first 280 miles travel at 70 mph by truck, then the remaining distance at 35 mph by local delivery. What is the total delivery time?
Answer: (C) 8 hours
Truck: 280/70 = 4 hrs. Local: 140/35 = 4 hrs. Total = 8 hours.
