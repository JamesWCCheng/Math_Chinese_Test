🚀 SAT Math: Rate, Speed & Distance

Strengthen your foundation — 10 Questions

Distance = Rate × Time

💡 Key Formulas to Remember

d = r × t  →  r = d / t  →  t = d / r
Average Speed = Total Distance ÷ Total Time (NOT the average of two speeds!)
1Multiple Choice
A car travels 180 miles in 3 hours. What is the car's average speed, in miles per hour?
Answer: (D) 60
Speed = Distance ÷ Time = 180 ÷ 3 = 60 mph.
2Multiple Choice
If a cyclist rides at a speed of 15 km/h, how far will the cyclist travel in 2 hours and 40 minutes?
Answer: (C) 40 km
2 hours 40 minutes = 2 + 40/60 = 8/3 hours.
Distance = 15 × 8/3 = 40 km.
3Multiple Choice
A train travels from City A to City B at 80 mph and returns at 120 mph. If the distance between the two cities is 240 miles, what is the average speed for the entire round trip?
Answer: (B) 96 mph
Time going = 240 ÷ 80 = 3 hours. Time returning = 240 ÷ 120 = 2 hours.
Total distance = 480 miles. Total time = 5 hours.
Average speed = 480 ÷ 5 = 96 mph.
⚠️ Note: It is NOT (80+120)/2 = 100. You must use total distance ÷ total time!
4Multiple Choice
Maria walks to school at 3 mph and the trip takes 20 minutes. If she rides her bike at 12 mph instead, how many minutes will the trip take?
Answer: (C) 5 minutes
Distance = 3 × (20/60) = 1 mile.
Time by bike = 1 ÷ 12 = 1/12 hour = 5 minutes.
5Multiple Choice
Two cars start from the same point and travel in opposite directions. Car A travels at 55 mph and Car B travels at 65 mph. After how many hours will they be 360 miles apart?
Answer: (C) 3 hours
Combined rate = 55 + 65 = 120 mph (they move apart).
Time = 360 ÷ 120 = 3 hours.
6Multiple Choice
A delivery truck left a warehouse at 8:00 AM traveling at 40 mph. A second truck left the same warehouse at 10:00 AM traveling at 60 mph along the same route. At what time did the second truck catch up to the first?
Answer: (B) 2:00 PM
By 10:00 AM, the first truck traveled 40 × 2 = 80 miles.
Speed difference = 60 − 40 = 20 mph.
Time to catch up = 80 ÷ 20 = 4 hours after 10:00 AM = 2:00 PM.
7Multiple Choice
Water flows into a pool at a rate of 5 gallons per minute. The pool holds 1,200 gallons and is currently 1/4 full. How many minutes will it take to fill the rest of the pool?
Answer: (D) 180 minutes
Remaining = 1200 × 3/4 = 900 gallons.
Time = 900 ÷ 5 = 180 minutes (= 3 hours).
8Multiple Choice
A plane flies 600 miles with a tailwind of 50 mph in exactly 2 hours. What is the plane's speed in still air?
Answer: (B) 250 mph
Effective speed = 600 ÷ 2 = 300 mph.
Effective speed = still air speed + tailwind → 300 = s + 50.
Still air speed = 250 mph.

Sam drives from his house to the office at 30 mph during rush hour and returns home at 50 mph. If the total round trip takes 2 hours and 40 minutes, what is the distance (in miles) between his house and the office?
Answer: 50 miles
Let d = distance. Total time = d/30 + d/50 = 2 hours 40 min = 8/3 hours.
(5d + 3d) / 150 = 8/3 → 8d / 150 = 8/3 → d = 50 miles.

A runner completes the first half of a 28-mile race at 8 mph and the second half at 6 mph. What is the runner's average speed for the entire race? (Round to the nearest hundredth.)
Answer: 6.86 mph
First 14 miles at 8 mph: time = 14/8 = 1.75 hours.
Second 14 miles at 6 mph: time = 14/6 ≈ 2.333 hours.
Total = 28 miles in ≈ 4.083 hours.
Average speed = 28 ÷ 4.083 ≈ 6.86 mph (exactly 48/7).
⚠️ Common mistake: (8+6)/2 = 7 is WRONG. Always use total distance ÷ total time!
