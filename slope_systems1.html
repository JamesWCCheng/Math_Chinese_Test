📊 SAT Math: Systems & Slope — Set 1

Linear Equations, Slope, and Intersections

y = mx + b  |  m = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁)

💡 Key Concepts

Slope = rise/run = Δy/Δx
Parallel lines → same slope  |  Perpendicular lines → slopes multiply to −1
1MC
What is the solution to the system: 2x + y = 7 and x − y = 2?
Answer: (A) (3, 1)
Add the equations: 3x = 9 → x = 3, y = 1.
2MC
What is the slope of the line passing through (2, 5) and (6, 13)?
Answer: (B) 2
m = (13−5)/(6−2) = 8/4 = 2.
3MC
The equation 3x − 4y = 12 represents a line. What is its slope?
Answer: (C) 3/4
Rewrite: −4y = −3x+12 → y = (3/4)x − 3. Slope = 3/4.
4MC
A system of equations has the solution (2, −3). Which system could it be?
Answer: (A)
Check: 2+(−3)=−1 ✓, 2(2)−(−3)=7 ✓.
5MC
What is the y-intercept of the line with slope 3 that passes through (2, 8)?
Answer: (C) 2
y = 3x+b → 8 = 6+b → b = 2.
6MC
Two lines: y = 2x + 1 and y = −x + 7. At what point do they intersect?
Answer: (B) (2, 5)
2x+1 = −x+7 → 3x = 6 → x = 2, y = 5.
7MC
Line ℓ passes through (0, 4) and (3, 0). What is the equation of line ℓ?
Answer: (A)
m = (0−4)/(3−0) = −4/3. y-int = 4. y = −4/3 x + 4.
8MC
If lines y = mx + 3 and y = 2x − 1 are parallel, what is m?
Answer: (D) 2
Parallel → same slope. m = 2.
9Free ResponseGrid-In
Solve: 3x + 2y = 16 and 5x − 2y = 24. What is x?
Answer: x = 5
Add: 8x = 40 → x = 5. Then y = (16−15)/2 = 1/2.
10MC
A line perpendicular to y = 3x + 1 passes through (6, 1). What is its equation?
Answer: (A)
Perp slope = −1/3. y−1 = −1/3(x−6) → y = −1/3 x + 2 + 1 = −1/3 x + 3.
