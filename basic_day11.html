1MC
Solve for x:
⅔(x - 1) - ¾(x + 2) = -2
Answer: (B) -2
Multiply all terms by 12 to clear fractions:
8(x - 1) - 9(x + 2) = -24.
Expand: 8x - 8 - 9x - 18 = -24 → -x - 26 = -24.
Add 26 to both sides: -x = 2 → x = -2.
2MC
Solve the system of equations for y:
2(x - 1) - 3(y - 2) = 5
3(x + 1) + 2(y - 1) = 9
Answer: (A) 1
Simplify Equation 1: 2x - 2 - 3y + 6 = 5 → 2x - 3y = 1.
Simplify Equation 2: 3x + 3 + 2y - 2 = 9 → 3x + 2y = 8.
Multiply the first by 2: 4x - 6y = 2.
Multiply the second by 3: 9x + 6y = 24.
Add them: 13x = 26 → x = 2.
Substitute x = 2 into Eq 1: 2(2) - 3y = 1 → 4 - 3y = 1 → -3y = -3 → y = 1.
3MC
Solve the inequality for x:
4(x - 1) - 3 ≥ 2(x + 1) + 5
Answer: (C) x ≥ 7
Expand both sides: 4x - 4 - 3 ≥ 2x + 2 + 5 → 4x - 7 ≥ 2x + 7.
Subtract 2x: 2x - 7 ≥ 7.
Add 7: 2x ≥ 14.
Divide by 2: x ≥ 7.
4MC
A man rows a boat upstream at a speed of 4 km/h and downstream at a speed of 8 km/h. If he rows a total distance of 16 km upstream and 16 km downstream, how many hours does the entire trip take?
Answer: (C) 6
Time spent rowing upstream = 16 km / 4 km/h = 4 hours.
Time spent rowing downstream = 16 km / 8 km/h = 2 hours.
Total time = 4 + 2 = 6 hours.
5Free Response
Working together, a master painter and an apprentice can paint a fence in 4 hours. The master painter works at exactly twice the speed of the apprentice. How many hours would it take the apprentice working alone to paint the fence?
Answer: 12
Let r = work rate of the apprentice per hour. Then the master's rate = 2r.
Their combined rate = r + 2r = 3r per hour.
We are given their combined time is 4 hours, which means their combined rate is 1/4 of the fence per hour.
Set up the rate equation: 3r = 1/4 → r = 1/12.
Since the apprentice's rate is 1/12 of the fence per hour, it takes the apprentice 12 hours alone.
