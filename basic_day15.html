1MC
Solve for x:
(0.5x - 1)/3 - (0.2x + 1)/2 = -0.7
Answer: (B) 2
Multiply all terms by 6 to clear denominators:
2(0.5x - 1) - 3(0.2x + 1) = -4.2.
Expand: x - 2 - 0.6x - 3 = -4.2 → 0.4x - 5 = -4.2.
Add 5 to both sides: 0.4x = 0.8.
Divide by 0.4: x = 2.
2MC
Solve the system of equations for x:
(x - y)/2 + (x + y)/3 = 1.5
(2x + y)/3 - (x - 2y)/4 = 5/3
Answer: (B) 2
Multiply Equation 1 by 6: 3(x - y) + 2(x + y) = 9 → 5x - y = 9 → y = 5x - 9.
Multiply Equation 2 by 12: 4(2x + y) - 3(x - 2y) = 20 → 8x + 4y - 3x + 6y = 20 → 5x + 10y = 20.
Substitute y into simplified Eq 2: 5x + 10(5x - 9) = 20 → 5x + 50x - 90 = 20.
Simplify: 55x = 110 → x = 2.
(Substituting x = 2 back gives y = 1).
3MC
Solve the system of inequalities for x:
(2x - 1)/3 ≥ (x + 1)/2
(3x + 2)/4 > (2x - 3)/2
Answer: (A) 5 ≤ x < 8
Solve inequality 1 (multiply by 6): 2(2x - 1) ≥ 3(x + 1) → 4x - 2 ≥ 3x + 3 → x ≥ 5.
Solve inequality 2 (multiply by 4): 3x + 2 > 2(2x - 3) → 3x + 2 > 4x - 6 → 8 > x (or x < 8).
Combine the ranges: 5 ≤ x < 8.
4MC
Two cyclists start at the same time from the same point and ride in opposite directions. One cyclist rides at an average speed of 18 km/h, and the other rides at 22 km/h. How many hours will it take for them to be exactly 100 km apart?
Answer: (B) 2.5
Since they ride in opposite directions, their relative separation speed is the sum of their individual speeds.
Relative speed = 18 + 22 = 40 km/h.
Time to be 100 km apart = Total separation distance / Relative speed = 100 km / 40 km/h = 2.5 hours.
5Free Response
Working together, Pipe A and Pipe B can fill a swimming pool in 6 hours. If Pipe A is opened alone for 4 hours, and then closed, and Pipe B is opened alone for 9 hours, the pool will also be filled. How many hours would it take Pipe A working alone to fill the pool?
Answer: 10
Let a = Pipe A's rate per hour, b = Pipe B's rate per hour.
Working together: 6a + 6b = 1 → a + b = 1/6 → b = 1/6 - a.
Sequential work: 4a + 9b = 1.
Substitute b: 4a + 9(1/6 - a) = 1 → 4a + 1.5 - 9a = 1.
Simplify: -5a + 1.5 = 1 → -5a = -0.5 → a = 0.1 (or 1/10).
Since Pipe A's rate is 1/10 of the pool per hour, it takes Pipe A 10 hours alone to fill the pool.
