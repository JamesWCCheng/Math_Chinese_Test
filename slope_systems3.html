Intercepts, Real-World Systems, and Graph Analysis

1MC
The sum of two numbers is 24 and their difference is 6. What is the larger number?
Answer: (C) 15
x+y=24, x−y=6. Add: 2x=30 → x=15.
2MC
What is the slope of a horizontal line?
Answer: (A) 0
Horizontal line: no rise → slope = 0. (Vertical line has undefined slope.)
3MC
Which point lies on the line y = 4x − 7?
Answer: (A) (1, −3)
Check: y = 4(1)−7 = −3 ✓.
4MC
In a parking lot, there are motorcycles (2 wheels) and cars (4 wheels). There are 15 vehicles and 50 wheels total. How many cars are there?
Answer: (C) 10
m+c=15, 2m+4c=50. Sub: 2(15−c)+4c=50 → 30+2c=50 → c=10.
5MC
Two lines intersect at (3, −2). One line is y = x − 5. The other has slope −1. What is its equation?
Answer: (B) y = −x + 1
y−(−2) = −1(x−3) → y+2 = −x+3 → y = −x+1.
6MC
For what value of k does kx + 2y = 6 and 3x + y = 4 have the solution x = 2?
Answer: (C) 5
From 2nd: 6+y=4 → y=−2. Sub into 1st: 2k+2(−2)=6 → 2k=10 → k=5.
7MC
A line with slope 2/3 passes through the origin. Which point is on this line?
Answer: (A) (3, 2)
y = (2/3)x. At x=3: y=2 ✓.
8MC
The graph of 2x − 3y = 6 crosses the x-axis at which point?
Answer: (B) (3, 0)
Set y=0: 2x=6 → x=3. Point = (3, 0).

Solve: 2x + 5y = 1 and 3x − 2y = −8. What is y?
Answer: y = 1
Multiply 1st by 3, 2nd by 2: 6x+15y=3, 6x−4y=−16. Subtract: 19y=19 → y=1.
10MC
A line passes through (−1, 6) and (2, 0). What is the x-intercept?
Answer: (B) 2
The line passes through (2, 0), which IS the x-intercept since y=0 there. x-int = 2.
