⚖️ SAT Math: Variation — Set 2

Applied Proportion Problems

Direct: y/x = k  |  Inverse: xy = k
1MC
A car uses 4 gallons of gas to travel 120 miles. At the same rate, how many gallons are needed for 300 miles?
Answer: (C) 10
Rate = 120/4 = 30 mpg. Gallons = 300/30 = 10.
2MC
The speed of a gear is inversely proportional to the number of teeth. Gear A has 20 teeth and rotates at 60 rpm. Gear B has 30 teeth. What is Gear B's speed?
Answer: (B) 40 rpm
k = 20×60 = 1200. Speed = 1200/30 = 40 rpm.
3MC
On a map, 2 cm represents 50 km. Two cities are 7 cm apart on the map. What is the actual distance?
Answer: (B) 175 km
Scale: 50/2 = 25 km/cm. Distance = 7×25 = 175 km.
4MC
If y varies inversely as the square of x, and y = 4 when x = 3, what is y when x = 6?
Answer: (B) 1
y = k/x². k = 4×9 = 36. y = 36/36 = 1.
5MC
A shadow of a 6-foot person is 4 feet long. At the same time, a tree casts a shadow of 20 feet. How tall is the tree?
Answer: (C) 30 ft
6/4 = h/20 → h = 6×20/4 = 30 ft.
6MC
It takes 5 machines 8 hours to complete a job. How many hours would 4 machines take?
Answer: (C) 10
Inverse: k = 5×8 = 40. Hours = 40/4 = 10.
7MC
The price of fabric is directly proportional to its length. If 3.5 meters cost $28, what is the cost of 5 meters?
Answer: (C) $40
Rate = 28/3.5 = $8/m. Cost = 5×8 = $40.
8MC
The frequency of a vibrating string is inversely proportional to its length. A 60 cm string vibrates at 440 Hz. What length vibrates at 330 Hz?
Answer: (D) 80 cm
k = 60×440 = 26400. Length = 26400/330 = 80 cm.

y varies directly as x. When x = 7, y = 42. What is x when y = 78?
Answer: x = 13
k = 42/7 = 6. x = 78/6 = 13.
10MC
Which graph represents an inverse variation y = k/x for k > 0?
Answer: (C)
y = k/x (k>0) is a hyperbola in quadrants I and III. (A) is direct variation.
