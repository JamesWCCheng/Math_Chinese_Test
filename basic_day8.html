1MC
Solve for x:
3[2 - 4(x - 1)] = -18
Answer: (C) 3
Divide both sides by 3: 2 - 4(x - 1) = -6.
Subtract 2: -4(x - 1) = -8.
Divide by -4: x - 1 = 2.
Add 1: x = 3.
2MC
For what value of k will the system of equations have infinitely many solutions?
3x - 2y = 4
kx - 6y = 12
Answer: (C) 9
For a system to have infinitely many solutions, the two equations must represent the same line (their coefficients and constants must be proportional).
Compare y-coefficients: -6 / -2 = 3. So the second equation must be 3 times the first.
Check constants: 4 × 3 = 12 (matches).
Solve for k: 3 × 3 = 9.
3MC
Which range represents the solution to the system of inequalities:
4x + 1 ≥ 9
2x - 7 > 3x - 12
Answer: (A) 2 ≤ x < 5
Inequality 1: 4x ≥ 8 → x ≥ 2.
Inequality 2: Subtract 2x and add 12 to both sides: 5 > x (or x < 5).
Combine: 2 ≤ x < 5.
4MC
Six years ago, a father was exactly 4 times as old as his daughter. In 12 years, he will be exactly twice as old as his daughter. How old is the daughter now?
Answer: (C) 15
Let F = father's age, D = daughter's age.
6 years ago: F - 6 = 4(D - 6) → F = 4D - 18.
In 12 years: F + 12 = 2(D + 12) → F = 2D + 12.
Set equations equal: 4D - 18 = 2D + 12 → 2D = 30 → D = 15.
5Free Response
An airplane flies 1,200 miles with a tailwind in 4 hours. On the return trip, flying against the same wind (headwind), the flight takes 5 hours. What is the speed of the wind in miles per hour?
Answer: 30
Let p = speed of plane in still air, w = speed of wind.
Tailwind speed: p + w = 1,200 / 4 = 300 mph.
Headwind speed: p - w = 1,200 / 5 = 240 mph.
Subtract the second equation from the first: (p + w) - (p - w) = 300 - 240.
2w = 60 → w = 30 mph.
