1MC
A store sells apples for $2 each and bananas for $1 each. Sam buys 8 fruits and spends $11. How many apples did he buy?
Answer: (B) 3
a+b=8, 2a+b=11. Subtract: a = 3.
2MC
What is the slope of the line 5x + 2y = 10?
Answer: (A) −5/2
2y = −5x+10 → y = −5/2 x + 5.
3MC
The lines y = 3x − 2 and y = 3x + 4 are:
Answer: (B) Parallel
Same slope (3), different y-intercepts → parallel.
4MC
At a concert, adult tickets cost $12, child tickets $8. 200 tickets were sold for $2,080 total. How many adult tickets were sold?
Answer: (C) 120
a+c=200, 12a+8c=2080. Substitute: 12a+8(200−a)=2080 → 4a=480 → a=120.
5MC
What is the equation of a line with slope −2 and y-intercept 5?
Answer: (B) y = −2x + 5
y = mx+b → y = −2x + 5.
6MC
For the system x + 3y = 10 and 2x + 6y = 15, how many solutions exist?
Answer: (A) 0
Second equation simplifies to x+3y=7.5, which contradicts x+3y=10. No solutions (parallel lines).
7MC
What is the midpoint of the segment connecting (−2, 5) and (8, −3)?
Answer: (A) (3, 1)
M = ((−2+8)/2, (5+(−3))/2) = (3, 1).
8MC
Person A earns $8/hr and has $20 saved. Person B earns $5/hr and has $65 saved. After how many hours will they have the same amount?
Answer: (C) 15
20+8h = 65+5h → 3h = 45 → h = 15.

If the line passing through (1, k) and (4, 10) has slope 2, what is k?
Answer: k = 4
(10−k)/(4−1) = 2 → 10−k = 6 → k = 4.
10MC
Solve: 3x − y = 5 and x + 2y = 4. What is x + y?
Answer: (B) 3
From 2nd: x=4−2y. Sub: 3(4−2y)−y=5 → 12−7y=5 → y=1, x=2. x+y=3.
